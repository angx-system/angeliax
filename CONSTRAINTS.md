# ANGX — System Constraints

These ten constraints apply to every layer of the system simultaneously. Every decision at every level runs through them.

---

**1. Alignment** — No signal or connection serves any purpose other than honest recording of real work and real surplus. No growth incentive, no attention mechanism, no ranking layer.

**2. Autonomy** — No central authority controls who participates, what is recorded, or how bases connect. Every base steward runs their base independently and decides which feeds enter their collection.

**3. Verification** — No entry exists without a cryptographic signature from its author. Authorship is always provable, always traceable to a keypair.

**4. Propagation** — What is logged in one base is reachable by every base connected to it through the partner chain. Knowledge that enters the network stops dissolving.

**5. Non-ownership** — No institution or steward owns what another has built, logged, or freely provides. Bases hold copies, authors hold originals. The keypair is the only authority over a feed.

**6. Replication** — No feed exists in one place only. Every device that replicates a feed holds a full copy. The more devices hold a copy, the more resilient that knowledge becomes.

**7. Resilience** — No single node or base is load-bearing. If a base goes offline, its feeds survive on every device that ever replicated them. The network continues regardless of what any single part does.

**8. Transparency** — No entry can be hidden, edited, or deleted after it is written. Contradictions sit alongside corroborations forever with equal visibility.

**9. Locality** — No base is a global mirror and no base is interchangeable with another. Each base holds what is relevant to its location and region. Every connected base is reachable through the partner chain.

**10. Contact** — No node is admitted to a base collection and no base is initialized without physical observation of the work or provision involved. That observation may be performed directly by the base steward or established through a sufficient witness record from verified stewards who were physically present. Partner handshakes require mutual trust between base stewards, established through direct meeting or independent assessment of each other's collection record.

The claim is cryptographic. The act is not. A base that admits nodes without adequate observation produces unverifiable collections that partner bases notice and stop propagating. Enforcement is structural and reputational.
